Marketing & Growth

The growth engine for Product OS

Strategy, messaging, and growth work for SynergyOS.ai. Everything here feeds into our Product Brain — the single source of truth for how we talk about, position, and grow the product.

North Star Metric

First Time Good %

The percentage of work that ships correctly, coherently, on the first attempt.

Why this metric drives everything

First attempt = final attempt

Every re-run, every "that's not how we do it," every rework cycle is waste. Product OS exists to eliminate that waste by giving your AI (and your team) the Critical Context it needs to get it right the first time. When First Time Good % goes up, rework goes down, velocity goes up, and teams get time back to think deeply — or simply rest.

Core Messaging

Three pillars of the Product OS story

Every piece of marketing content should reinforce one or more of these pillars.

1

Critical Context is the moat

Only Product OS delivers the right information, at the right time, in the right quantity to an LLM. No prompt engineering, no wiki spelunking, no context windows stuffed with irrelevant noise. Your AI just knows your product.

2

Effortless setup, compounding value

Five minutes to install. Zero maintenance. The more your team uses it, the richer the context becomes, the better the output, the more people want to use it. Growth is built into the product mechanics.

3

Build coherently, ship confidently

Product OS replaces scattered wikis, tribal knowledge, and prompt gymnastics with a living knowledge graph. The result: coherent products built by teams that aren't drowning in rework.

What is the Product Brain?

The Product Brain is the living knowledge graph behind SynergyOS — the same system Product OS MCP gives your AI access to. Marketing insights, positioning decisions, audience learnings, and growth experiment results all get captured as structured entries with typed relations.

This means our marketing knowledge compounds just like product knowledge. A positioning decision from six months ago stays connected to the messaging pillar it influenced, the content it spawned, and the growth metric it moved.
